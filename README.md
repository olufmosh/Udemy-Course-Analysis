# Udemy-Course-Analysis
The dashboard offers comprehensive insights into course popularity by subject, the engagement of learners, and the effectiveness of different levels of content

# Udemy Courses Report Overview
## Total Subscribers, Reviews, and Courses:

Total Subscribers: 12 million users are subscribed to Udemy courses.

Total Reviews: 574,000 reviews across all courses, indicating active engagement and feedback from learners.

Total Courses: 3,672 courses are available on the platform, covering a wide variety of topics.

## Course Popularity by Subject:

Web Development is the most popular subject, followed by Business Finance, Graphic Design, and Musical Instruments.

This highlights a strong interest among learners in developing technical skills, particularly in web development, while business and design courses also attract significant attention.

## Most Popular Courses by Subscribers:

"Learn HTML5 and CSS3 from Scratch" is the most subscribed course, with significantly higher subscriber numbers compared to other popular courses like:

"Coding for Entrepreneurs"

"The Web Developer Bootcamp"

"Build Your First Website"

"The Complete Web Developer"

This suggests high demand for beginner-friendly web development courses, particularly focusing on HTML5 and CSS3.

## Subscribers by Course Level:

The "All Levels" category dominates in terms of subscribers, indicating that most learners prefer courses designed for a wide range of proficiency levels.

Beginner Level courses are the second most popular, reflecting the demand for foundational courses in areas such as web development and business finance.

Intermediate and Expert Levels have comparatively fewer subscribers, showing that most learners are still in the early stages of their learning journey.

## Subscriber Trends by Month:

The Monthly Subscription Trend graph shows fluctuations in subscriber numbers, with a noticeable peak in June. This suggests a seasonal trend, possibly due to increased interest in courses during the summer months, such as students taking advantage of their vacation time.

Web Development remains the subject with the most consistent and largest subscription numbers, reinforcing its ongoing popularity.

## Key Observations for Actionable Insights:

Focus on Beginner Web Development Courses: With the high number of subscribers to beginner-level web development courses, Udemy could continue promoting or expanding courses that focus on HTML5, CSS3, and related technologies.

Course Quality Monitoring: Regularly monitoring course ratings can help identify underperforming courses. For instance, the low ratings of the "Photoshop CS5" course may indicate the need for content updates or improvements in the teaching approach.

Targeted Marketing for High-Demand Subjects: Given the popularity of subjects like Web Development and Business Finance, Udemy could drive further engagement through targeted marketing campaigns in these areas.

Leverage Seasonal Trends: The spike in subscriptions during June suggests that Udemy could capitalize on this trend by offering promotions or campaigns tailored to the summer months.
